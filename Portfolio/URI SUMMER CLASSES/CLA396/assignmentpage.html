Topic

  • Discuss the relationship between gods and men as you see it in the texts which we have read this session. What is myth?  How does myth aid men and women in their understanding of the gods?  Do myths bring people closer to the gods? Choose one myth or scene from any of the three mythological texts we have read – Vergil’s Aeneid, Ovid’s Metamorphoses, Apuleius’ Golden Ass – and analyze it, given the anthropological tools we have acquired from Scheid’s An Introduction to Roman Religion. Here are a few examples:
    • Aeneas is considered pious.  In order to be pious, Aeneas has to walk a fine line of appeasing the gods and fulfilling his duty, his fate.  Choose one scene from the Aeneid in which he both fulfills his duty and appeases the gods.
      • Aeneas’ trip to the Underworld 
      • the funeral games for Anchises
      • the treaty and funeral for the death of Pallas
      • Aeneas’ encounters with his mother (there are two)
    • There is a three-part omen/oracle manifestation in Book 7 – the beehive in the laurel tree, Lavinia’s hair catches fire, and Picus gives Latinus advice.  Discuss this.  What are the implications of the omens and oracles?
    • In Bk I of Ovid, the scene of Deucalion and Pyrrha after the Flood
    • Arachne and Minerva or Niobe and Latona
    • Daedalus and Icarus, Phaethon and Helios
    • Medea's prayer to Hecate (religious worship or magical incantation?)
    • Orpheus and Eurydice
    • Lucius’ accidental transformation into a donkey by drinking a love potion and his resulting worship of the cult of Isis.
    • The Myth of Cupid and Psyche
    • These are just a few examples, there are many others.  Feel free to draw from the forums to develop your thesis.
